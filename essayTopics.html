Essay Topics

  • Originally Jane Austen had planned to title the novel First Impressions, how would this have been a suitable title?
  • title fits the novel perfectly just as the final title.
  • First impressions play a major role, and in Darcy and Elizabeth's cases they must overcome his first impressions of Elizabeth in order for there relationship to succeed.
  • Initially Darcy sees Elizabeth as not attractive enough and too homely and dismisses her, he also initially has a class based prejudice towards her as she is not wealthy enough and lacks the family connections that he believes his future wife should have.
  • Elizabeth on the other hand first sees Darcy as an arrogant and snobbish man, which allows her to easily believe untrue slanderous accusations about Darcy.
  • The two must overcome these first impressions in order to realize their true love for one another.
  • Darcy manages to put aside his prejudice, and Elizabeth comes to see Darcy's true character, and the relationship blossoms.
  • Does Mr. Bennet play a positive or negative role in the novel?
  • Austen portrays Mr. Bennet as an extremely witty and interesting man.
  • He is an endearing character due to his wit, as well as detachment from the some of the other more ridiculous characters such as Mr. Collins or Lady Catherine.
  • Despite his amusing nature and likability he lacks important qualities and doesn't prove to be a very good husband or father.
  • This is further proved when the controversy between Lydia's elopement occurs, Mr. Bennet is unable to handle the situation and instead it is left up to others.
  • This experience goes to show that despite being a witty and dynamic character, he lacks the important qualities that would make him a positive and respected character.
